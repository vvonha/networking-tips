"Your network is your net worth." But only if it's genuine.

Before Events

Set a goal. "Meet 3 new people" is better than "network."
Research attendees. Know who'll be there. Have talking points ready.
Prepare your intro. 30 seconds: who you are, what you do, what you're looking for.

Starting Conversations

"What brought you here today?" Better than "What do you do?"
Give genuine compliments. "I loved your talk on X" opens doors.
Ask for opinions. "What do you think about [industry trend]?"
Join groups of 3+. Easier than interrupting 1-on-1 conversations.

During Conversations

Listen more than you talk. 70/30 rule. Ask follow-up questions.
Remember names. Repeat it back: "Nice to meet you, Sarah."
Be genuinely curious. People sense fake interest immediately.
Find ways to help. "I know someone you should meet" is powerful.
Don't pitch immediately. Build rapport first. Sales comes later.

After Events

Follow up within 24-48 hours. Reference something specific you discussed.
Connect on LinkedIn. With a personalized note, not the default.
Offer value first. Send an article they'd find useful. Make intro you promised.
Stay in touch. Periodic check-ins. Don't only reach out when you need something.

Online Networking

Comment thoughtfully. Not "Great post!" Add real insights.
Share others' content. With your own take. Tag them.
DMs work. Be specific about why you're reaching out.
Build in public. Share your work, learnings, journey.
